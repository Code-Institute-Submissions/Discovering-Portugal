Serra da Estrela

"In summer or winter, mainland Portugal's highest mountain is the perfect scenery for a few relaxing days in contact with nature."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Serra da Estrela is a place directed for natural tourism

Here there are several Hotels and local accommodations to choose from, I would highly recommend Casa de São Lourenço - Burel Mountain Hotels or H2otel Congress & Medical Spa

The option to simply camp out in the nature is also available in one of the many camping sites there present. I would recommend trying the Parque de Campismo Rural Vale do Beijames or Valhelhas Camping

Serra da Estrela has several water courses and fluvial beaches that are breathtaking. If you choose to visit this area of Portugal do not miss the fluvial beach of Loriga and the fluvial beach of Vale do Rossim

This place is home to some amazing typical food such as Serra da Estrela Cheese and "Enchidos" (different types of Chorizo and Sausages), don't miss it

Serra da Estrela has several marked tracks, all of them breathtaking so just walk around and get lost in the natural beauty of this place

National Park of Peneda-Gerês

"The Serra da Peneda and Serra do Gerês constitute the only Portuguese protected area that has been classified as National Park. It is a world apart, where human activity is integrated in a harmonious manner with nature, preserving ancient values and traditions that can be seen in the community villages of Pitões das Júnias and Tourém."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Gerês is a place directed for natural tourism

Here there are several Hotels and local accommodations to choose from, I would highly recommend the Hotel São Bento or the Tempus Hotel & Spa

The option to simply camp out in the nature is also available in one of the many camping sites there present. I would recommend trying the Ermida Gerês Camping or Parque Cerdeira

This place has several water courses and fluvial beaches that are breathtaking. If you choose to visit this area of Portugal do not miss the Tahiti waterfall and the Pincães waterfall

The National Park of Peneda-Gerês has several marked tracks, all of them breathtaking so just walk around and get lost in the natural beauty of this place

Porto

"Capital and gateway to the north of Portugal, Porto is both the city that provided a nation with a name and a fortified wine known world-wide: port. (...) Any visitor to Porto will be quickly impressed by the forceful character both of the city and it's inhabitants."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Porto is a place directed for city tourism

Here there are several Hotels and local accommodations to choose from, I would highly recommend the Pestana Palácio do Freixo or the Flores Village Hotel & Spa

Porto is home to some amazing typical food such as "Francesinha" and "Tripas à Moda do Porto", don't miss it

When visiting Porto, besides the amazing museums and monuments the city has to offer, be sure to walk around the Aliados and Ribeira areas and to cross the Bridge of D. Luís to Gaia to visit the Port wine cellars and see the amazing view to Porto

Aveiro

"The capital of the Ria, a vast lagoon where the freshwater of the River Vouga joins with the sea, Aveiro is intersected by canals, genuine streets of water, along which can be seen gliding the brightly colored boats known as barcos moliceiros. (...) Aveiro is now one of the most interesting cities on the Portuguese coast."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Aveiro is a place directed for city tourism

Here there are several Hotels and local accommodations to choose from, I would highly recommend the Hotel Aveiro Palace or the Hotel das Salinas

Aveiro is home to some amazing typical food such as "Ovos Moles de Aveiro" and "Tripas Doces", don't miss it

When visiting Aveiro, besides the amazing museums the city has to offer, be sure to take a ride through the Ria on one of the typical boats, called "Moliceiros" and to visit the "Salinas" (place where sea salt is extracted)

Costa Nova do Prado Beach

"With the sea often rough and much appreciated by surfers, Costa Nova do Prado Beach covers a large expanse heading off southwards in the direction of Vagueira Beach, excellent for those liking a long walk along the seashore. On the other side of the coast road, Aveiro Estuary, with its calm waters, is a favorite for sailing and windsurfing in what constitutes an entirely different natural environment."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Costa Nova is a place directed for beach tourism

Here the best accommodations are without a doubt one of the typical striped houses that are available to rent, such as Belle View Apartment

In Costa Nova you will find Camping Costa Nova, it is not fantastic, but it's still a better option than Barra Camping

Praia da Costa Nova is a blue flagged beach (considered by the authorities a clean beach) and is watched by lifeguard

Moledo Beach

"In an area of great natural beauty, surrounded by the Camarido protected area, this Beach is somewhat windy with strong waves reaching shore here creating good year-round surfing conditions and windsurfing during the summer."

Quote from VisitPortugal, see full article here


Moledo Beach is a place directed for beach tourism

This beach in itself does not offer any accommodations, however close by are several amazing options, from which I would highly recommend Villa Idalina or Prazer da Natureza Resort & Spa

Moledo Beach in itself does not have a camping site, however the Parque de Campismo Orbitur Caminha is very close by and definitely worth it

Moledo Beach is a blue flagged beach (considered by the authorities a clean beach) and is watched by lifeguard
